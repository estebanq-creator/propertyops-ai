## Executive Summary

Three major competitors announced significant pricing and feature changes effective Q1 2026. The market is shifting toward **tiered pricing models** and **AI-differentiated features**. Key trend: competitors are monetizing AI capabilities rather than using them as retention tools.

**PropertyOps AI Implication:** Our Phase 0-1 positioning (AI-native ops platform for 5-150 unit landlords) remains defensible if we maintain discipline on Laura (forensic analysis, not screening) and Tony (human-in-loop execution). ## Pricing Changes (Q1 2026)

### AppFolio
**Change:** Shifted from unit-based ($1.40-1.75/unit) to tiered model with base fees + per-unit charges.

| Tier | Portfolio | Base/Mo | Per-Unit | Example (100 units) |
|------|-----------|---------|----------|---------------------|
| Core | ≤50 units | $250 | $2.00 | $450/mo |
| Plus | 51-500 | $500 | $1.75 | $1,750/mo |

**Impact:** 15-25% average increase. Vacant units now charged at 50% rate (was 0%). Small portfolios (<20 units) face new $99/mo minimum (was free tier).

**Source:** AppFolio Nov 2025 press release, industry analysis (Capterra, G2, Software Advice Jan 2026)

## Strategic Implications for PropertyOps AI

### Threats
1. **Buildium Tenant Insights** blurs line between retention and screening. If they expand to admission/eviction recommendations, they may trigger Fair Housing scrutiny — but could also confuse market expectations about what AI can legally do.
2. **AppFolio price increases** may push small landlords (20-50 units) to seek alternatives — but they're also the most price-sensitive. Our pricing must account for this.
3. **DoorLoop inspection automation** sets customer expectations for AI capabilities we don't yet match in physical ops.

### Opportunities
1. **Compliance Posture as Differentiator:** Buildium's Tenant Insights and AppFolio's "AI screening" create exposure. We can position Laura as **forensic-only** (no scoring, no recommendations) — safer for landlords worried about Fair Housing liability.
2. **Human-in-Loop Discipline:** Tony's requirement for human approval (Phase 1-2) is a feature, not a limitation. Competitors are automating collections and maintenance assignment — we can position our approach as **audit-ready** and **defensible**.
3. **Price Gap:** Buildium Essential at $62/mo for ≤20 units is the floor. AppFolio's new $99/mo minimum for small portfolios creates whitespace. If we target $75-125/mo for 5-50 units with AI-native ops, we're competitive.

### Recommended Actions
1. **Week 2:** Deep-dive AppFolio AI screening claims — get product demos or user reports to understand actual functionality.
2. **Week 2:** Review Buildium Tenant Insights TOS and compliance documentation — assess Fair Housing exposure.
3. **Week 3:** Customer interviews — ask prospects how they perceive competitor AI features vs. our positioning.
4. **Ongoing:** Monitor DoorLoop for acquisition signals (they're well-funded, may be M&A target for larger player).
