## Strategic Implications for PropertyOps AI

### Competitive Advantages (Phase 1)

1. **Price Point:** Targeting 5–150 unit landlords at ~$99–$199/month (under development) — significantly below Buildium Premium ($400) and AppFolio minimums ($280+).

2. **Compliance Posture as Feature:** Laura's forensic-only constraint (no scoring, no screening recommendations) is a **differentiator**, not a limitation. We can market this as "Fair Housing-safe AI" vs. competitors' black-box recommendations.

3. **Human-in-the-Loop Default:** Tony's human-approval requirement (Phase 1–2) aligns with Buildium's approach but at lower price point. AppFolio's autonomous agents may create liability exposure we can exploit in messaging.

4. **Memory & Feedback Moat:** Competitors lack our structured memory compounding (every interaction improves future performance with audit trail). This is a switching cost advantage.

### Threats to Monitor

1. **AppFolio Down-Market Move:** If AppFolio reduces minimums or launches a "Starter" tier, they could crush us on brand recognition. **Monitor:** Pricing page changes, job postings for SMB sales roles.

2. **Buildium AI Democratization:** If Buildium moves Write with AI or Bill Scan to Essential tier ($62/month), our price advantage shrinks. **Monitor:** Tier feature matrix updates.

3. **New Entrant with AI-Native Positioning:** A well-funded startup could replicate our forensic + operational model. **Monitor:** Product Hunt, TechCrunch, venture funding announcements in proptech AI.
