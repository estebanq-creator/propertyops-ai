## 4. Strategic Implications

### Threats
1. **Braiin positioning:** AI-native framing could commoditize PropertyOpsAI's intelligence layer if they target same 5-150 unit segment. Need to monitor their pricing and customer profile.
2. **Buildium Lumina AI:** Adding AI features to established SMB platform. If they price AI as add-on (not tier upgrade), could undercut dedicated AI solutions.
3. **RealPage rent risk scoring:** $39/unit/year bad debt reduction claim is specific, measurable ROI. PropertyOpsAI needs comparable outcome metrics for Laura (document integrity) and Tony (maintenance triage).

### Opportunities
1. **AppFolio minimums create gap:** $280/mo minimum excludes landlords under ~200 units on Core plan. PropertyOpsAI can serve 5-150 unit segment AppFolio ignores.
2. **Buildium AI is assistive, not forensic:** Lumina focuses on task efficiency (bill scan, message drafting). Laura's forensic document integrity positioning is distinct—no scoring, no screening recommendations. This is a compliance feature, not a replacement for human judgment.
3. **Fair Housing compliance as differentiator:** Competitors (RealPage, TenantCloud) emphasize predictive risk scoring. PropertyOpsAI explicitly rejects scoring/screening recommendations. This is a constraint-as-feature play for compliance-conscious landlords.

### Market Positioning Clarity
**PropertyOpsAI vs. Competitors:**
| Dimension | AppFolio | Buildium | RealPage | PropertyOpsAI |
|-----------|----------|----------|----------|---------------|
| Target | 50+ units | SMB tiers | Enterprise multifamily | 5-150 units |
| AI Approach | Embedded (maintenance) | Assistive (Lumina) | Predictive scoring | Forensic + Operational |
| Pricing | $1.40-3.00/unit, high minimums | ~$39/mo+ | Custom enterprise | TBD |
| Compliance Posture | Standard | Standard | Risk optimization | Fair Housing first |
